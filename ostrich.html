What is OSTRICH?

OSTRICH (Observation System Tracking Radiation Incidence through Correlation Heuristics) is a cosmic ray research experiment with the aim to study the arrival direction of cosmic rays in the arctic. The experiment is scheduled to fly aboard the BEXUS 37 high-altitude balloon as part of the REXUS/BEXUS programme, supported by SNSA, DLR, and ESA.

The primary goal of the OSTRICH mission is to validate the design of a multi-layer cosmic ray detector in the stratosphere, simulating the environment of space. This system is intended as a precursor to a future CubeSat-based detector. Its scientific purpose is to:

  • Measure the flux of cosmic rays as a function of altitude
  • Determine the angle of arrival of high-energy particles
  • Test the feasibility of generating a sky-map of cosmic ray directions
The mission also included long-term reliability tests of the onboard electronics, data storage, calibration systems, and communication links, all within the extreme conditions of near-space.

Launch & Results

OSTRICH is scheduled to launch from Esrange Space Center, near the city of Kiruna in Sweden, in October 2025. It will float at an altitude of around 30 kilometers for at least an hour, gathering valuable scientific data.
